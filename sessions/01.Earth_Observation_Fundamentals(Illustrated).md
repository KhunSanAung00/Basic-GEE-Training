# Earth Observation Fundamentals (Illustrated)

## 1. How Remote Sensing Works

 
![3.png](assets\3.png)

**Explanation**
1. The Sun emits energy.
2. Energy reaches the Earth's surface.
3. Different surfaces reflect different amounts of energy.
4. Satellites measure the reflected energy.
5. We convert the measurements into useful information.

---

## 2. Electromagnetic Spectrum

![4.png](assets\4.png) 

Humans can only see visible light (Blue, Green, Red).

Satellites can also measure:

- Near Infrared (NIR)
- Shortwave Infrared (SWIR)
- Thermal Infrared

These additional wavelengths allow us to monitor vegetation, water, soil moisture, drought, and wildfires.

---

## 3. Spectral Signatures

![5.png](assets\5.png) 

Every land cover type has a unique spectral signature.

### Vegetation
- Reflects strongly in NIR
- Absorbs red light for photosynthesis

### Water
- Absorbs most NIR and SWIR energy
- Appears dark in infrared imagery

### Urban Areas
- Reflect visible and infrared differently from vegetation
